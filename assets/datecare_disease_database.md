## Specimen 01: BLACK SCORCH
**Scientific Name:** *Ceratocystis paradoxa*

### 1. What is it?
Black Scorch is a common fungal disease that affects all parts of the date palm. It typically leads to blackened, charred appearances on fronds, fruit stalks, and occasionally the trunk. It is highly contagious within an orchard if not properly managed.

### 2. Why did it occur?
* **High Humidity:** Excessive moisture creates an ideal fungal environment, allowing spores to germinate rapidly.
* **Poor Pruning:** Unsanitary tools easily spread highly infectious spores between healthy and sick trees during routine maintenance.
* **Wounded Tissue:** Physical damage from pests, weather, or careless handling provides direct entry points for the fungus.

### 3. Restoration Protocol
1. **Prune & Purge:** Remove all infected fronds and blackened tissue immediately. Burn or safely dispose of the material far from the orchard.
2. **Copper-Based Fungicide:** Apply a systematic copper spray thoroughly to the crown, trunk, and surrounding soil to halt fungal growth.
3. **Sterilization:** Disinfect all tools, gloves, and equipment with a 10% bleach solution immediately after use to prevent further spread.

### 4. Treatment Duration
14 Days

## Specimen 02: BAYOUD DISEASE
**Scientific Name:** *Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. albedinis*

### 1. What is it?
A lethal soil-borne fungus that attacks the vascular system of the date palm. It is considered one of the most destructive diseases in date palm history, causing the tree to dry out from the inside out, usually starting with a single frond.

### 2. Why did it occur?
* **Contaminated Soil:** The fungus can survive in the soil for decades, spreading through water runoff or movement of earth.
* **Infected Offshoots:** Using offshoots from a parent tree that unknowingly carries the fungus.
* **High Salinity:** Stress from high salt levels in the soil can weaken the tree's natural immunity to the fungus.

### 3. Restoration Protocol
1. **Quarantine:** Immediately isolate the area. Do not move soil or plant material out of the infected zone.
2. **Soil Solarization:** Cover the soil with transparent plastic sheets during the hottest months to kill the fungus with trapped heat.
3. **Replacement:** If the tree is lost, replace it only with certified resistant cultivars like 'Sair' or 'Tadment.'

### 4. Treatment Duration
90 Days

## Specimen 03: DIPLODIA
**Scientific Name:** *Diplodia phoenicum*

### 1. What is it?
A fungal infection particularly dangerous to young palms and offshoots. It mimics the symptoms of Black Scorch but focuses its attack on the base of the leaves and the trunk's growth points.

### 2. Why did it occur?
* **Excessive Moisture:** Over-irrigation or poor drainage creates a damp environment at the base where the fungus thrives.
* **Over-Pruning:** Removing too many green fronds at once stresses the tree and creates numerous open wounds.
* **Poor Airflow:** Trees planted too close together prevent the wind from drying out the crown after rain.

### 3. Restoration Protocol
1. **Surgical Pruning:** Carefully cut away dead or dying tissues until only healthy, white inner tissue is visible.
2. **Fungicide Application:** Use a targeted fungicide drench specifically at the site of the pruning wounds.
3. **Drainage Adjustment:** Re-grade the soil around the palm to ensure water flows away from the trunk base.

### 4. Treatment Duration
21 Days

## Specimen 04: DUBAS BUG
**Scientific Name:** *Ommatissus lybicus*

### 1. What is it?
A tiny, sap-sucking insect (leafhopper) that feeds on the fronds. Their waste, known as "honeydew," coats the leaves and encourages the growth of black sooty mold, which blocks photosynthesis.

### 2. Why did it occur?
* **High Tree Density:** Orchards with crowded canopies provide the shaded, humid environment these bugs prefer.
* **Low Predators:** A lack of natural enemies like ladybugs or lacewings due to over-use of broad-spectrum pesticides.
* **Warm Temperatures:** Extended periods of warm, dry weather can cause a population explosion of the nymphs.

### 3. Restoration Protocol
1. **Monitoring:** Regularly check the underside of fronds for sticky residue or small yellow spots.
2. **Biological Control:** Introduce or encourage natural predators to manage the population naturally.
3. **Systemic Spray:** If the infestation is severe, use a targeted systemic insecticide that only affects sap-sucking insects to minimize environmental impact.

### 4. Treatment Duration
7 Days
